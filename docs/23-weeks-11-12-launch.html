Weeks 11-12 — Soft Launch & Iterate

This is the end of the v2.0 build. The product is feature-complete, hardened, and measurable; now it meets real users. We launch softly — to a known group of campus and hiring-manager pilots, on an opt-in beta channel — watch the metrics, fix what breaks, and make a clear-eyed go/no-go against the Week-12 success criteria. No big-bang launch; the auto-update channel flips to default only after the beta runs four weeks bug-free.

0 · How to use this document

Same conventions as the prior phase docs. This phase is launch operations: readiness, a controlled rollout, instrumentation-watching, and a decision. The team holds, the founder sells; interns do not pair on sales.

1 · Where we are right now

  • From W9-10: CI green and signed/notarized on both platforms, P0/P1 burned down, performance within budget, telemetry and a feedback channel live, security gates closed.
  • The app updates via an opt-in beta channel (how v1 users find v2). The plan is to flip auto-update to default after four weeks bug-free.
  • Recipients: a known set of pilots — existing v1 users, a handful of strangers who tested v1, and new students via 1-2 campus/bootcamp partnerships, plus the first hiring-manager pilots Namkha brings in.

2 · Launch-readiness checklist

Do not ship to a pilot until every box is checked:

  • ☐ Signed + notarized installers on macOS and Windows; auto-update verified on both.
  • ☐ Zero open P0/P1 bugs.
  • ☐ Fresh-install onboarding reaches first capture in under four minutes.
  • ☐ Telemetry live for weekly actives, training opt-in rate, and frames-with-consent.
  • ☐ A feedback/support channel exists and is monitored.
  • ☐ Stripe live-mode tested; a real card can subscribe and the portal works.
  • ☐ Consent + GDPR delete verified end to end; T&C and DPIA in place.

3 · The phase at a glance

  • Group A: onboarding polish + first-run funnel instrumentation watching.
  • Group B: the auto-update channel-flip mechanics + crash reporting.
  • Group C: auth edge cases + GDPR account-deletion under real users.
  • Namkha: campus + HM pilots, sales, and the scorecard / go/no-go.

4 · Group A — Frontend (Jacqueline)

Mission. Get real users through the front door and learn where they drop.

  • Polish onboarding against real pilot feedback; cut friction in the path to first capture.
  • Watch the first-run funnel telemetry; turn drop-off points into small, fast fixes (not new features).
  • Keep the five-screen scope frozen — pilot requests go to the v2.1 backlog.

Done-when. Pilots complete onboarding without hand-holding; funnel drop-off is visible and the worst step is improved.

5 · Group B — Desktop/IPC & release (Lihan)

Mission. Own the release mechanics and see crashes before users report them.

  • Implement the auto-update channel flip: opt-in beta now, default after four weeks bug-free. Make the flip a config change, not a code change.
  • Crash reporting: capture and surface renderer/main crashes from pilots.
  • Keep both-platform release artifacts healthy across the pilot.

Done-when. A new build reaches beta-channel users via auto-update; crashes are reported centrally; the channel-flip is a one-switch operation.

6 · Group C — Auth & Secure Storage (Yingzhu)

Mission. Auth and data-deletion that survive contact with real users.

  • Harden auth edge cases pilots hit (cold tokens, refresh failures, multi-device).
  • Verify GDPR account deletion works for a real pilot who asks for it, end to end.
  • Watch sign-in success-rate telemetry; fix the top failure mode.

Done-when. No auth incidents in the pilot; a real deletion request is honored within the retention window; sign-in success rate is healthy.

7 · Namkha — pilots, sales, scorecard

  • Run the campus/bootcamp partnerships to bring in students; make the first warm hiring-manager intros and pilot offers.
  • Land the first paid HMs (founder-sales — interns do not pair).
  • Own the success-metric scorecard and the go/no-go decision.
  • Run blameless postmortems for any Sev-1/Sev-2 during the pilot.

8 · Success-metric scorecard & go/no-go

At the end of Week 12, score against the v2.0 targets and decide:

  • ☐ 100+ weekly-active workers (60% from outside the founder network).
  • ☐ 5 paid hiring managers (~$1.5k MRR).
  • ☐ 80% of free users opted in to training-data sharing.
  • ☐ 1M+ labeled frames collected with consent. [CONFIRM with Namkha: this is feasible from ~100 weekly actives over the window — sanity-check against capture volume and the perceptual-dedup rate.]
  • ☐ Zero capture-pipeline bug reports in the final four weeks.
  • ☐ 30+ student users via 1-2 campus partnerships.

Then the call: go (flip auto-update to default, widen the beta), hold (extend the pilot, fix the gating issue), or pivot (the validation assumptions did not hold — adjust before scaling). Write the decision down.

9 · What v2.1 picks up

v2.0 ends here. The deferred items roll into v2.1 (months 4-6): the promo-packet generator, the weekly summary email, LinkedIn/Google OAuth, saved searches for HMs, the in-desktop Job Post UI continuation, and the public-profile redesign follow-through. v2.2 (months 7-9) is the B2B internal-reporting product and the first evaluation of the OnlyWorks fine-tuned model. None of that is in scope now — ship v2.0 first.

10 · When something is wrong

During a pilot, a Sev-1 is all-hands and pauses the rollout; do not flip the auto-update channel with an open Sev-1. Pilot feature requests are backlog, not this-week work. Keep the go/no-go honest — a metric you cannot measure is a no, not an assumed yes.
