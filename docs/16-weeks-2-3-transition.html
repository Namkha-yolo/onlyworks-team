Weeks 2-3 — Transition & Carry-Over

Week 2's plan and its status are captured in doc 15 and doc 18. The full Week 3-4 plan is doc 19. This short doc is the bridge between them: what has to close from Week 2 and what starts in Week 3, so nothing falls into the gap. It is a live action doc, not a plan — read it, do the thing, check the box.

0 · What this document is

Foundation is not cleanly "done" at the Week-2 line. Two of three groups shipped their Week-2 slice; one (auth) is behind; and the capture engine — the thing all of Week 3-4 hangs off — has not started. This doc names the carry-over explicitly and sequences the first moves of Week 3 so the team does not stall waiting on each other. When the carry-over boxes in §3 and §8 are all checked, you are fully into doc 19.

1 · Where we are right now

On main of OnlyWorksCodeHub/onlyworks_desktop_v2 at the end of Week 2:

  • Group A (Jacqueline) — shipped. Shared contracts, the @shared alias, and the Status page wired to the typed capture client (useMutation / useQuery + error Dialog) are merged.
  • Group B (Lihan) — shipped. The hand-rolled tRPC-over-IPC bridge is merged; capture.start / capture.status work as an in-memory stub. The bridge decision doc is Accepted.
  • Group C (Yingzhu) — behind. The auth contracts exist and the v1 auth services are ported into src/main, but there is no auth.router.ts on the bridge, no electron-store, and no email-code views. Auth is not usable yet. This is the carry-over that matters most.
  • CaptureEngine — not started. capture.ts is still the stub whose own comment says the real engine "lands in W3." That is now.
  • Housekeeping: two dependencies (electron-log, electron-updater) still carry caret ranges and should be exact-pinned; CI is macOS-only.

2 · The transition in one paragraph

Group C finishes email-code auth so every downstream authenticated screen is unblocked; Namkha stands up the CaptureEngine and — the pivot of the whole phase — publishes the FrameEvent contract and the capture.events subscription early in Week 3; the moment that lands, Group B starts the OcrConsumer and Group C (after auth) starts the TrainingDataConsumer against it. Group A keeps the momentum on onboarding. Everything in Week 3-4 depends on those two events — auth finishing and the capture handoff landing — happening early, not late.

3 · Week-2 carry-over checklist

These are Week-2 items that are not done and must close in the first days of Week 3:

  • ☐ Group C: email-code auth on the bridge (the big one — see §6).
  • ☐ Pin electron-log and electron-updater to exact versions (drop the carets).
  • ☐ [CONFIRM with Namkha: who owns adding the Windows CI lane — start it now, build-only, rather than discovering Windows broken in Week 9.]
  • ☐ Confirm the capture-contract handoff date so B and C can plan around it (§7).

4 · Group A — Jacqueline · now

You are ahead — keep the lead useful. Your Week-2 slice is merged, so use this transition to set up for capture and onboarding without blocking on anyone.

  • Against the FrameEvent contract shape Namkha is about to publish, prepare the Status page to consume the capture.events subscription (build behind the agreed shape; wire it for real the moment the subscription lands).
  • Start the First-run onboarding route as a step shell (welcome → permissions → sign-in → consent → first-capture → done) — navigation only for now.
  • Do not get ahead of the design system or add screens beyond the five; this is setup, not the W5-6 build.

Done-when. Status is ready to bind to the real subscription on day one of its availability; the onboarding shell navigates end to end.

5 · Group B — Lihan · now

Bridge is done — turn to OCR and the platform gap.

  • Read v1 src/services/ocr-service.js and stand up an OcrConsumer skeleton that registers against the (about-to-be-published) engine consumer interface. Build behind the contract until the subscription lands.
  • Add the Windows CI lane now (build-only, no signing). Doing it here instead of Week 9 means Windows breakage surfaces on every PR from the start.
  • Coordinate with Namkha on where the macOS Accessibility precheck + TCC relaunch live, since both your OCR path and his engine touch it.

Done-when. OcrConsumer skeleton compiles against the agreed contract; a Windows build-only CI job runs on PRs.

6 · Group C — Yingzhu · now (the priority)

Finish auth. This is the single most important carry-over. Until it lands, A's onboarding sign-in step and every authenticated screen downstream are blocked, and you cannot start the TrainingDataConsumer.

  • Add src/main/trpc/auth.router.ts with auth.requestEmailCode, auth.verifyEmailCode, auth.status, auth.signOut; mount it under appRouter (coordinate the mount with Lihan).
  • Wire POST {OW_BACKEND_URL}/api/auth/email and .../email/verify (base URL from Namkha, not hardcoded).
  • Encrypt tokens with electron-store + safeStorage; never write a raw token to disk; read expiry from the JWT exp claim; refresh on powerMonitor 'resume'.
  • Build the 6-digit code views (react-hook-form + zod). Mind the token-shape traps (verify nests under data.tokens, refresh nests flat; a 429 returns error as a string).

Done-when. A real email-code sign-in works end to end against staging; tokens are encrypted at rest; sign-out wipes them. (Then you roll into the TrainingDataConsumer in doc 19.)

7 · Namkha — publish the capture handoff (the pivot)

The transition turns on one deliverable: the capture contract handoff, early in Week 3. Until it lands, B's OcrConsumer and C's TrainingDataConsumer cannot be wired for real.

  • Replace the capture.ts stub with the real CaptureEngine driving capture.start / capture.status.
  • Publish the typed FrameEvent in src/shared/contracts/capture.contract.ts (replace the placeholder), the capture.events subscription on the bridge, and the consumer-registration interface.
  • Announce it in #swe-team the moment it is on main.

The engine spec, the parameters to lock, and this handoff's day-by-day live in your private notes (not in the intern docs).

8 · Definition of done for the transition

  • ☐ Group C email-code auth works end to end; tokens encrypted at rest.
  • ☐ Namkha has published the FrameEvent contract + capture.events subscription.
  • ☐ Group B's OcrConsumer skeleton registers against the contract; Windows CI lane exists.
  • ☐ Group A's Status is ready to bind to the subscription; onboarding shell navigates.
  • ☐ Caret deps pinned; Windows-CI owner confirmed.

When these are checked, the team is fully into doc 19 (Weeks 3-4).

9 · Risks watch list

  • Auth slipping further. It is already behind; if it is not done in the first days of Week 3, onboarding and every authenticated screen slip with it. It is Group C's sole focus until it lands.
  • The capture handoff landing late. If Namkha's contract slips, B and C have nothing to build against. Protect the early-Week-3 date; flag any slip in the Tuesday standup.
  • Windows deferred again. Adding the CI lane now is cheap; discovering Windows broken in Week 9 is not.

10 · Then what

This doc exists to be finished quickly. Once the §8 boxes are checked, stop reading it and work from doc 19, which carries the full Weeks 3-4 plan — the consumers, consent, OCR on both platforms, and the onboarding build — through to the end-of-Week-4 demo. Surface blockers in standup and #swe-team; never merge red; write decisions in the PR.
