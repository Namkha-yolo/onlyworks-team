OnlyWorks — PMF Analysis and v2 Direction

This document defines who v2 is for, what to build, and what to defer. Reflects: (a) the team's existing direction (verified portfolio + hiring marketplace), (b) the v2 roadmap (already documented), (c) the backend reality (Stripe gap, half-built verification loop, real-user talent community), and (d) the founder's preference to lean on Path B (brag doc / promo packet) and Path C (verified portfolio) over Path A (billing clerk).

Headline Positioning

OnlyWorks captures your work. Your work becomes a verified portfolio, your status reports write themselves, and hiring managers find you.

One product. One underlying capture engine. Three distinct output channels for three distinct revenue streams.

  • D2C marketplace (free workers + paid HMs): Workers build verified portfolios passively; hiring managers pay to search + reach them.
  • D2C utility (workers / Pro): Promo packets, weekly summaries, public profile — career-builder tools layered on top of the portfolio.
  • B2B internal reporting (team subscription, per-seat): Your team's captured work becomes their auto-generated status reports, eliminating "what did you do this week?" friction. The team feature in v1 was always for this — just never framed clearly.

Three jobs, one engine:

  • Capture once → portfolio + status reports + promo packets + marketplace signal, all derived from the same underlying frames.

This is the unification of Paths B + C + hiring marketplace + the team feature (re-positioned). Path A (billing/invoice automation) remains deferred to v3 or skip.

Why This Combination

Path B (brag doc / promo packet) = the active worker use case. Quarterly/annual reviews. Promo cycles. Justifying raises. The Lattice/Workday review-prep workflow. Real demand exists; brag-doc workflows are now standard at FAANG.

Path C (verified portfolio) = the passive worker use case. A profile that builds itself from real work. Sharable to anyone. The "career resume that's actually true."

Hiring marketplace = the monetization. Workers don't pay (or pay tiny amounts). Hiring managers pay 100−500/mo for search access + job posts + outreach.

These three reinforce each other:

  • The promo packet gives workers a daily reason to keep the desktop running (otherwise capture quality degrades on review-prep day).
  • The verified portfolio is the artifact that promotes and the asset that gets searched.
  • The hiring side monetizes the artifact without charging workers.

Compare to Path A standalone (billing clerk):

  • Strong wedge, validated by PointOne, 30−80/mo ARPU per user.
  • But: requires a real product pivot. The current portfolio + hiring stack becomes dead weight. The team has built ~80% of B+C+hiring; only 20% of A.
  • The founder also indicated uncertainty about A's implications.

Where Path A Survives in v2

Path A (AI billing) does not disappear — it becomes a feature for the freelancer subset of the ICP, shipped in v2.1 or v3:

  • Tag sessions to clients (manual or auto-detected from screen content)
  • Generate invoice line items from captured sessions
  • Export to CSV / QuickBooks / FreshBooks

It's a side door for the 10-20% of users who are billing-by-hour freelancers. It doesn't drive the marketing, the pricing, or the architecture.

Why this matters: the team can validate whether billing is a real wedge later, without committing the entire company to it now. If 30% of v2 free users want billing integrations, build Path A as a $19/mo add-on. If 5% do, skip it.

Target User (ICP)

One unifying frame: people actively building their career who want hiring managers to find them — across two life stages.

Primary segment A: Students and early-career professionals (0–5 years)

The sharpest, highest-intent segment.

Concretely:

  • Status: undergraduate/graduate students, bootcamp students, recent grads, first/second-job employees, interns
  • Behavior pattern: building projects (hackathons, capstone projects, school assignments, side projects, internships, first jobs)
  • The job: translate "I built a thing" into "here are the verified skills I demonstrated" → show to HMs
  • Hiring stage: actively seeking first job, internship, or next role
  • Tech profile: comfortable with desktop apps and permissions; lives in VS Code / Figma / Notion / browser tabs; aware of the resume-building hustle
  • No employer concerns about installing screen-capture (they're often not employed, or it's their own machine)
  • Time-rich, money-poor — high engagement potential, low individual willingness to pay
  • Why they install: "If OnlyWorks watches me build my projects, it will auto-build my portfolio and connect me with employers." This is the explicit value prop.

Examples of the canonical user (the "Brodey" persona):

  • CS undergrad building a hackathon project who wants to show recruiters what they did
  • Design student working on senior thesis who wants verified evidence of Figma + research skills
  • Bootcamp grad with 3 portfolio projects looking for their first job
  • Intern at a startup wanting to track and verify what they shipped over the summer

Primary segment B: Mid-career knowledge workers (3–15 years experience)

The deeper, more durable segment.

  • Status: Currently employed individual contributors and middle managers at tech-adjacent companies
  • Career disposition: "Always open to better opportunities" — passive job-seekers, the ~70% of LinkedIn users who haven't quit but would talk
  • The job: maintain a verified career portfolio that makes them findable + ready for promo cycles
  • Activation events: promo cycle, performance review, manager change, layoff fear, recruiter ping
  • Tech profile: macOS-first (~70%) / Windows (~30%); Discord + Notion + Figma + GitHub natives

Both segments converge on the same product. The desktop captures work, the AI extracts verified skills, the portfolio gets seen by HMs.

Secondary: hiring managers and recruiters

  • Pays for: search access, job posting, Express Interest outreach
  • Sweet spot: companies that hire for digital roles, especially open to early-career talent (where verified-by-capture skills beat the noisy signal of GPA + GitHub + resume embellishment)

Out of scope (deliberate)

  • Solo freelancers billing hourly → Path A, defer to v3
  • Manager-watching-team workflows → bossware reputation risk
  • Enterprise compliance buyers → 4-person team can't sell enterprise
  • Job seekers in non-digital industries → tooling fit is weak
  • Established senior leaders (Staff+, Director+) who don't need verified skills

Value Proposition (worker side)

Three things workers get, in priority order:

  1. "Your work history, captured for you." A verified portfolio that builds itself. Run OnlyWorks while you work or build → AI extracts skills, accomplishments, projects → portfolio updates automatically. This is the base layer. For students this is the entire reason to use it: prove you actually built the thing.

  2. "Get found, not searched out." When the right opportunity matches your verified profile, you get a private alert from a hiring manager. No application spam, no LinkedIn shoutouts. This is the passive job-seeker hook — and for students, it's the "internship/first-job dream": HMs come to you with verified evidence of your skills.

  3. "Your promo packet, ready every Friday." (Mid-career segment.) Every week, OnlyWorks summarizes what you shipped, what skills you demonstrated, and what to talk about in your 1:1. When promo season hits, you have 26-52 weekly summaries already written.

The student-flavored value prop: "Build your project with OnlyWorks running. Get a verified portfolio + skill profile that hiring managers can actually trust. Get reached out to instead of applying."

The mid-career-flavored value prop: "A verified career history that's always current. Compounds while you work. Get found by recruiters for what you actually do, not what you wrote on LinkedIn."

Value Proposition (hiring side)

For HMs and recruiters:

  1. "Search verified candidates, not LinkedIn profiles." Skills are derived from actual captured work — not self-claims. Match scores reflect frequency + recency of demonstrated skill.

  2. "See evidence before you message." Every candidate has expandable evidence per skill — which projects, which apps, what they actually did. This is the "GitHub for non-developers" pitch.

  3. "Reach passive candidates respectfully." Express Interest is async, candidate-controlled, can't be spammed. Opt-out per HM is one-click.

The differentiator stack:

  2. Privacy-first (on-device OCR, sensitive-content filtering, no team views) → defuses the bossware reflex
  3. Passive portfolio → no journaling discipline required, works for the 90% who don't blog
  4. Single ICP narrative → "mid-career digital ICs and managers" beats "anyone who works"

Pricing

Three tiers. Worker side is mostly free; revenue comes from hiring managers.

Worker tiers

Tier Price What's included Why
Free $0 Capture + portfolio + 1 weekly summary email + share public profile + appear in HM search (opt-out) Drive activation and supply for the hiring side. Students and most workers stay here.
Pro $9/mo annual / $12/mo monthly Unlimited summary emails + custom reports + sensitive-content controls + theme/personalization + export to PDF Pricey enough to filter signal users; cheap enough to be impulse
Student (Free Pro) $0 with .edu email verification Everything in Pro, free while enrolled Students are the highest-intent supply for the marketplace; remove price friction

Hiring manager tiers (D2C marketplace revenue)

Tier Price What's included
Free $0 Browse search teasers (anon hashed IDs), see talent community, no outreach
Starter $99/mo 5 active job posts, 25 Express Interest sends/mo, full verified-candidate search
Pro $299/mo Unlimited job posts, unlimited Express Interest, saved searches, candidate notes
Recruiter Team $599/mo 3 seats, shared shortlists, ATS integration (v2.1+)

Internal team reporting tiers (B2B per-seat — v2.2+, separate revenue stream)

Tier Price What's included
Team Starter $9/seat/mo (min 5 seats) Auto-generated weekly/sprint status reports, employee reviews + sends, Discord + email delivery, manager digest
Team Pro $19/seat/mo (min 10 seats) + Custom report templates, integrations (Jira, GitHub, Linear), 1:1 prep notes, OKR check-ins, admin controls
Team Enterprise Custom + SSO, audit logs, BAA, custom data retention

Compared to async-standup incumbents:

  • Geekbot (2.50–5/seat): manual answers in Discord
  • Range (6–8/seat): manual updates + retro
  • Friday: manual standups
  • OnlyWorks differentiator: reports written from actual captured work, not from "answer these 3 questions every Monday." That's a real time-saver (~30 min/week/employee).

Why this pricing:

  • Worker pricing is intentionally cheap because the worker is the supply. We lose money on Pro server costs at $9/mo but Pro buys signaling commitment + better data quality (less likely to install, capture nothing, churn).
  • HM pricing benchmarks against LinkedIn Recruiter Lite (170/mo), fullRecruiter(900+/mo), Hired (placement fees 15-25% first-year salary). Starter at $99 is a "try it" price; Pro at $299 is the bet.
  • CRITICAL: Stripe must be integrated before any of this matters. Today, the HM paid tier is gated by a subscription_status column that requires a manual DB update. No checkout flow exists.

Roadmap (v2.0 → v2.2)

Note: The team's existing v2 roadmap was already aligned with this direction. P0 (cost/quality) + P1 (revenue) are the right priorities. Below restructures around the rebuild context.

v2.0 — "Compounding Portfolio" (Months 1-3)

The daily hook (per founder gut check): passive portfolio that builds itself. Desktop is invisible-but-trustworthy; complexity lives on the web side. The worker doesn't need to engage daily — they need to trust that something good is being built.

Worker desktop side (~5 screens total, slim)

  • New app: Electron + React + TS + Vite + Tailwind + tRPC, built in onlyworks_desktop_v2/
  • Port: capture, OCR, auto-updater, magic-link auth, token refresh (see architecture review)
  • Screens:
    1. Status — capture is running, today's count, current session indicator
    2. Portfolio — verified skills + evidence, sortable, editable visibility
    3. Privacy — opt-in/out of training data, sensitive content rules, what-we-captured review reel
    4. Settings — account, appearance, sign-out, app version
    5. First-run — permission grants + magic-link auth + training data consent
  • Drop: Manager/team views, Goals UI, Activity page, Sessions page, dashboard greeting clutter, tutorial coach-marks (replace with empty-state copy)
  • Email magic link only (defer OAuth to v2.1)
  • Unified capture pipeline (per architecture review) — one CaptureEngine, multiple consumers: OCR for reports, TrainingDataConsumer for model-training data (consent-gated)

Web side (where marketplace UX lives)

  • Public profile (already exists, polish)
  • Hiring search (already exists)
  • HM dashboard with job posts + matches + Express Interest (per v2 roadmap P1)
  • HM Stripe checkout + billing portal

Backend changes

  • Stripe integration: Checkout + webhook + /api/hiring/subscribe + /api/hiring/portal (2-3 days) — #1 unblocker for revenue
  • Per-user AI cost cap across text + vision paths (1 day)
  • Talent community nurture worker (day-3/14/30 emails, ~1 day)
  • Training data storage + consent tracking (new endpoints for TrainingDataConsumer)
  • Clean up: delete mocked AIAnalysisService, /api/backtest, unused @pinecone-database/pinecone dep

Out of scope for v2.0

  • Assignment-based verification ("upload resume → assignment → AI grades" loop) — defer to v2.2 or skip entirely if real-work verification proves sufficient
  • Promo packet generation — defer to v2.1 (the daily hook is portfolio, not packet)
  • Weekly summary email — defer to v2.1; test whether the passive hook is enough first
  • Billing/invoice integrations (Path A) — defer to v3 or kill
  • Resume upload + AI parsing — only resurrect if assignment verification is built in v2.2

Training data milestone (long game):

  • v2.0 starts the data collection (consent-gated, from unified pipeline)
  • Target: 1M+ labeled frames within 12 months
  • At ~12 months: evaluate fine-tuning OnlyWorks-specific multimodal model to replace Gemini calls at scale
  • Strategic bet: Gemini is fine for v2.0–v2.2; own model unlocks better unit economics at >5k DAU

Success criteria for v2.0:

  • 100 weekly active workers (most outside the immediate network)
  • 5 paid hiring managers (500−1500 MRR)
  • Capture-to-profile pipeline working without bug reports for 4 weeks
  • 80%+ of free users opt-in to training data sharing (signal that the consent UX is clear)

v2.1 — "Promo Packet" (Months 4-6)

Worker side

  • New "Weekly Summary" email auto-sent every Friday (the active use case Path B promised)
  • New "Promo Packet" generator: select date range → generate management-friendly narrative (accomplishments framed for review committees, impact-first language)
  • Public profile redesign (better-looking, more shareable)
  • LinkedIn OAuth (if user research shows magic link is friction)

Backend changes

  • Talent Community nurture worker (day-3 / day-14 / day-30 emails)
  • Job match quality improvements (synonyms, fuzzy search per v2 roadmap P0)
  • Saved searches for HMs

Hiring side

  • Job Post UI in desktop (notifications when matches come in)

v2.2 — "Internal Reporting + Verification at scale" (Months 7-9)

B2B Team feature (the big v2.2 deliverable)

  • Team UI rebuilt with consent-first design — employee reviews their auto-generated status report before it's sent; manager sees aggregated digest, not raw captures
  • Status report templates: Weekly status, sprint retro, 1:1 prep, OKR check-in, quarterly review
  • Discord delivery (post-to-channel or DM)
  • Email delivery via Resend (already wired)
  • Team subscription via Stripe (per-seat, monthly billing, min seats)
  • Admin dashboard: invite team, set report cadence, view delivery status (NOT view raw captures)
  • Privacy guarantees in the product itself: every captured frame goes through the same sensitive-content filter; managers never see screenshots, only AI-synthesized summaries the employee has approved
  • Marketing positioning: "Stop writing status reports. Your AI writes them from your actual work, you review and send."

Worker side

  • (Optional) Assignment-based verification: upload resume → AI extracts unverified skills → generates assignments → user completes in OW → AI verifies. Only build this if the "captured work" verification proves insufficient for HM trust. Talk to 20 HMs first.

Backend changes

  • Move to Postgres trigram/jsonpath search (in-memory ow_profile_cache scan won't scale past ~5k candidates)
  • Move in-memory rate limits and magic-link store to Redis (single-instance scaling cliff)
  • Cost: per-user monthly AI budget enforcement
  • New team_subscriptions Stripe webhook handling (per-seat metered billing differs from HM tier)

What Must Survive (the non-negotiables)

  • Capture pipeline (algorithm intact, restructure into CaptureEngine + consumers)
  • OCR on-device (macOS Vision + Windows.Media.Ocr — drop Tesseract.js fallback)
  • Sensitive content filtering (sensitive-filter.js pattern — load-bearing for the privacy story)
  • Auto-updater (electron-updater + GitHub releases, port verbatim)
  • Auth + token refresh + sleep/wake handling (port verbatim)
  • macOS TCC permission relaunch trick (_launchTrustState, port verbatim)
  • electron-builder config (signing, notarization, NSIS, Mica)
  • verified_skills table + write path (durable storage that the OW Profile Rescue forced)
  • Three-layer session recovery (renderer rehydration + desktop orphan reconciliation + backend stale-session finalizer)
  • Forensic auditor prompt direction (don't let the AI hallucinate praise; verify, don't coach)

Backend Implications

Reflect findings from the backend audit. These must be addressed for v2 viability:

  1. Stripe integration is the #1 unblocker for revenue. ~2-3 days of work. stripe_customer_id and subscription_status columns already exist. Need: Checkout Session creation endpoint, customer.subscription.created webhook, billing portal link, gate /api/hiring/jobs to actual paid status. This is the only thing blocking v2.0 from earning a single dollar.

  2. The "+50 verified boost" only matters when there are real candidates. Today, ~14 users have populated portfolios. Until the desktop drives growth into the verified pool, hiring search returns near-empty results and the talent community side dominates. Plan v2.0 launch to coincide with a content/growth push (target: 500 verified workers within 90 days of launch).

  3. The talent community nurture worker is missing. Migration 018 added the columns (nurture_emails_sent) but no cron/worker exists. Without it, magic-link signups go cold. ~1 day to build with existing Resend setup.

  4. AI cost ceiling is precarious. Per-user vision cost cap exists ($0.50/day) but text reports don't have one. Stale-session finalizer can run full Gemini batches on abandoned sessions, doubling costs. Add a daily per-user AI budget across all paths (~1 day).

  5. Dead code to clean up. AIAnalysisService is mocked, /api/backtest is in-memory dev-only, @pinecone-database/pinecone is in deps but never imported. ~0.5 days of deletes.

  6. Skip the planned assignments / skill_verifications tables in v2.0. The current verified_skills table populated from real captured work is sufficient signal for HMs if the captured-work signal is good. Only build assignment-based verification if HM interviews reveal trust gap.

Risks and Open Questions

The biggest risk: the worker side is free, the hiring side requires marketplace liquidity. For HMs to pay, they need a pool of verified candidates worth searching. For workers to install + run capture daily, they need a reason — promo packet utility is the bet, but it's a quarterly/annual event, not daily.

Mitigations:

  • Make the weekly summary email the daily/weekly hook (compounding utility — every week of capture builds the portfolio).
  • The worker free tier is essential — don't gate the portfolio.
  • Seed the hiring search by inviting Brodey + the existing 14 users + every member of the founder's network as the first 50 verified candidates. The first 5 paid HMs will be friends and warm intros.

Open questions to test in the first 4 weeks:

  1. Do mid-career ICs actually install and keep running a passive capture tool? (Easy to test: ship v2.0 to internal network of 20, measure weekly active over 4 weeks.)
  2. Do HMs trust skills derived from captured work? Or do they bounce off "this seems weird, where does this data come from?" (Test with 20 HM interviews before building v2.1.)
  3. Does the promo packet narrative resonate, or is it too niche? (Test by offering it as a v2.0 free feature even though we said it's v2.1 — measure usage in last week of v2.0 beta.)
  4. Will users tolerate screen capture in 2026 (post-Recall, post-Rewind acquisition)? (Privacy story is load-bearing; test the messaging.)

Existential risks:

  • LinkedIn launches verified-by-capture (unlikely near-term — they have brand to protect)
  • Google/Microsoft bake verified work history into OS-level products (Microsoft Recall is dead, but the idea lives)
  • Regulatory pushback on workplace AI monitoring (EU AI Act kicks in Aug 2026 or deferred Dec 2027 — single-player positioning sidesteps "high-risk" classification but watch for changes)
  • Cost: Gemini per-user economics break at scale (current vision cap = $15/user/month, that's a meaningful margin hit on a $9/mo worker subscription)
